Santé

Stitched in the Southside

Omar Cruz grew up where concrete meets cumbia, on Chicago streets that taught him color, texture, and the weight of where you come from. Under the name Santé, he builds garments the way his city builds character: layer by layer, with nothing wasted. Every cut carries the pulse of Latino kitchens, weekend fútbol, and late-night sketch sessions that refuse to end at something safe. This isn't fashion designed to impress. It's fashion designed to remember.

His Latino roots don't sit on the surface as decoration; they run through the construction of every piece, the way a song's rhythm lives in the body long after it stops playing. Music, family, the particular light of a city that makes you earn its beauty. These aren't references. They're the raw material.

Santé Designs doesn't chase trends and has no interest in convincing anyone. Every piece is built with intention, cut with purpose, released when it's ready, never when a calendar says so. The work is quiet in its confidence and deliberate in its scarcity, made for people who already understand what they're looking at. If the clothes find you, they were probably meant to.

When he's not in the studio, Omar is on the pitch, across the table from family, or walking into a restaurant he's never tried, collecting textures, rhythms, and conversations that quietly find their way into every cut and colorway. The work doesn't start at the sketch; it starts at the life. Santé is building something that moves the way Chicago moves. Loud when it needs to be, precise when it counts, and there's always room at the table if you want to pull up.
